The Stock-In-Trade (SIT) page provides a comprehensive view of your inventory planning, helping you balance service levels with inventory costs.

## What is Stock-In-Trade?

Stock-In-Trade represents the inventory held across your distribution network. SIMCEL provides two perspectives:

| View | Description |
|------|-------------|
| **Unconstrained SIT** | Ideal inventory levels without supply constraints |
| **Constrained SIT** | Realistic inventory levels accounting for supply limitations |

Toggle between these views using the switch at the top of the page to understand the gap between your ideal and achievable inventory.

## What you'll see

### SIT chart
A visual representation of inventory levels over time. Select your preferred UOM (units, value, etc.) to view the data in the most meaningful way.

### SIT table
A detailed table showing inventory levels grouped by your organizational hierarchy. Expand rows to drill down from aggregate levels to individual product and location combinations.

## Key inventory KPIs

| KPI | Description |
|-----|-------------|
| **Unconstrained SIT** | Target inventory without supply constraints (month-end) |
| **Constrained SIT** | Expected inventory with supply constraints applied (month-end) |
| **Average Inventory Value** | Average monetary value of inventory held |
| **Average Inventory Volume** | Average physical quantity of inventory held |
| **DIO (Days Inventory Outstanding)** | Number of days of sales covered by current inventory |
| **Closing Stock (Gross Value)** | End-of-period inventory in monetary terms |
| **Closing Stock (Volume)** | End-of-period inventory in physical units |

<Note>
SIT values are calculated at month-end only, reflecting the closing inventory position for each period.
</Note>

## How to use it

1. **Compare constrained vs. unconstrained** - Toggle between views to understand where supply constraints impact your inventory
2. **Identify gaps** - Large differences between constrained and unconstrained SIT highlight areas where you may need to secure additional supply
3. **Monitor trends** - Watch inventory levels over time to prevent stockouts or excess inventory
4. **Check pending tasks** - The page shows if there are pending DRP (Distribution Requirements Planning) tasks that need to complete before data is fully available
