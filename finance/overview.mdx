The Finance module in SIMCEL provides a complete view of your financial performance through the **Business Explorer** dashboard. Track revenue, costs, margins, and profitability across your planning scenarios.

## Business Explorer

The Business Explorer is your financial command center. It consolidates all financial KPIs into a single dashboard with multiple widgets.

### What you'll see

#### KPI table widget
A comprehensive table showing all financial KPIs with values for each active scenario. Compare scenarios side by side to evaluate their financial impact.

#### P&L chart widget
A visual profit and loss breakdown showing how revenue flows through costs to arrive at profit metrics like Gross Profit, DPM, DPMA, and EBIT.

#### Sales and profit evolution chart
A time-series chart showing how sales and profit metrics evolve over your planning horizon. Spot trends, seasonality, and the impact of planned events.

#### KPI tracker table
A detailed table with hierarchical drill-down by product, customer, or location. Analyze financial performance at any level of granularity.

## Financial KPI structure

SIMCEL calculates financial KPIs following this P&L waterfall:

```
Gross FIE Sales
  - FIE Trade Expenses
= Net FIE Sales
  - Distributor COGS
= Gross Profit
  - Transport
  - Storage & Handling
= Supply Chain Cost
  - Promotion
  - Medical Affairs
= DPM (Distribution, Promotion & Medical)
  - Administration
= DPMA
= EBIT (Earnings Before Interest & Tax)
```

## Key financial KPIs

### Revenue metrics

| KPI | Description |
|-----|-------------|
| **Gross FIE Sales** | Total gross sales at factory import entry level |
| **Net FIE Sales** | Gross FIE Sales minus FIE Trade Expenses |
| **Gross Local Sales** | Total gross sales at local market level |
| **Net Local Sales** | Gross Local Sales minus Local Trade Expenses |

### Cost metrics

| KPI | Description |
|-----|-------------|
| **COGS** | Cost of Goods Sold |
| **Transport** | Transportation and logistics costs |
| **Storage & Handling** | Warehouse storage and handling costs |
| **Promotion** | Promotional activity costs |
| **Medical Affairs** | Medical affairs related expenses |
| **Administration** | Administrative overhead |
| **Supply Chain Cost** | Transport + Storage & Handling |

### Profitability metrics

| KPI | Formula | Description |
|-----|---------|-------------|
| **Gross Profit** | Net FIE Sales - Distributor COGS | Profit after cost of goods |
| **Gross Margin %** | (Gross Profit / Net FIE Sales) x 100 | Profitability as a percentage of net sales |
| **DPM** | Supply Chain Cost + Promotion + Medical Affairs | Distribution, promotion, and medical expenses |
| **DPM %** | (DPM / Net FIE Sales) x 100 | DPM as a percentage of net sales |
| **DPMA** | DPM + Administration | Total operating expenses |
| **DPMA %** | (DPMA / Net FIE Sales) x 100 | DPMA as a percentage of net sales |
| **EBIT** | Gross Profit - Transport - Storage & Handling - Promotion - Medical Affairs - Administration | Operating earnings |
| **EBIT %** | (EBIT / Net FIE Sales) x 100 | Operating margin percentage |
| **COGS %** | (COGS / Net FIE Sales) x 100 | Cost of goods as a percentage of net sales |

### Working capital metrics

| KPI | Description |
|-----|-------------|
| **AR (Accounts Receivable)** | Outstanding customer payments |
| **DSO (Days Sales Outstanding)** | Average days to collect payment |
| **DIO (Days Inventory Outstanding)** | Average days of inventory on hand |
| **CCC (Cash Conversion Cycle)** | DSO + DIO - days payable outstanding |

## Scenario comparison

The Business Explorer highlights differences between scenarios using color coding:

- **Green** - Favorable movement (higher revenue, lower costs)
- **Red** - Unfavorable movement (lower revenue, higher costs)

The percentage difference between scenarios is calculated as:

```
% Difference = ((Scenario Value - Active Scenario Value) / |Active Scenario Value|) x 100
```

<Note>
The direction of "favorable" depends on the KPI type. For revenue KPIs, an increase is favorable. For cost KPIs, a decrease is favorable.
</Note>
