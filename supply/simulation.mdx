SIMCEL's simulation engine (DRP - Distribution Requirements Planning) calculates how your supply chain performs under the demand and constraints defined in each scenario.

## What does a simulation do?

When you run a simulation, SIMCEL:

1. Takes the demand forecast from your scenario
2. Applies supply constraints (production capacity, lead times, inventory policies)
3. Calculates distribution requirements across your physical network
4. Determines what can be fulfilled and what cannot
5. Computes all supply, finance, and carbon KPIs from the results

## How to run a simulation

1. Navigate to **Supply Planning**
2. Select the scenario you want to simulate
3. Click the **Run Simulation** button
4. A progress dialog appears showing the simulation steps

## Simulation progress

The progress dialog shows each step of the simulation with real-time status:

| Status | Meaning |
|--------|---------|
| **Pending** | Step has not started yet |
| **Running** | Step is currently being processed |
| **Success** | Step completed successfully |
| **Error** | Step encountered an issue |

If an error occurs, you can expand the error message to see details about what went wrong.

<Warning>
Simulations can take several minutes depending on the size of your network and the number of products. Do not close the browser tab while a simulation is running.
</Warning>

## When to re-run a simulation

You should re-run your simulation when:

- You **add or modify events** in your scenario
- You **change demand assumptions** or apply demand shaping
- You **update master data** (products, facilities, network)
- You want to **refresh KPIs** after any planning change

## What gets calculated

After a successful simulation, the following are updated for the scenario:

- **Order fulfillment** rates and details
- **Inventory levels** (constrained SIT, closing stock)
- **Financial KPIs** (COGS, margins, P&L metrics)
- **Carbon emissions** (inbound, storage, outbound CO2)
- **All 51+ KPIs** across every dimension

<Tip>
Create multiple scenarios with different assumptions, run simulations on each, and use the scenario comparison features to evaluate which plan delivers the best outcomes.
</Tip>
